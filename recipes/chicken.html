Chicken Recipe

Description

Arroz caldo is a lugaw (glutinous rice dish) made with chicken and rice. It’s flavored with garlic, onions, ginger, fish sauce, and other seasonings.

Though “arroz caldo” is a Spanish name, the dish’s origins are Filipino — it is an adaptation of a congee that was created by Chinese-Filipino immigrants.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • cloves garlic, crushed
  • (2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • ¼ pounds chicken wings, split and tips discarded
  • tablespoon fish sauce (Optional)
  • ¼ cups chicken broth
  • cup glutinous sweet rice
  • alt and pepper to taste
  • green onion, chopped
  • lemon, sliced (Optional)
  • teaspoon fish sauce for sprinkling, if desired (Optional)

Instructions

  1. >Cook the onion, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. >Add the chicken and stir in the fish sauce.
  3. >Pour in the broth and sweet rice, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. >Season and continue cooking until the chicken is fully cooked.
